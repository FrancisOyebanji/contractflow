# Customer Discovery Plan — ContractFlow

## Objective

Validate that (a) contract cycle time is a top-3 operational pain for real estate operators, (b) AI clause review is trusted enough to change reviewer behavior, and (c) buyers will pay for workflow + intelligence rather than treating e-signature as a commodity.

## Interviewee mix (n=14 target)

| Segment | Count | Recruiting source |
|---|---|---|
| Transaction coordinators (multifamily & commercial) | 5 | Existing customer base, LinkedIn outreach |
| In-house counsel / paralegals | 3 | Industry association referrals |
| Portfolio / asset managers | 4 | Design partner intros |
| VP Operations (economic buyer) | 2 | Sales-sourced |

## Interview guide (45 min, semi-structured)

**Context (10 min)** — "Walk me through the last contract you closed, from first draft to signature. Who touched it, in what tools, and where did it sit the longest?"

**Pain depth (15 min)** — "When did a contract last surprise you — a clause, a deadline, a renewal? What did it cost?" Probe for frequency, workaround cost, and who feels the pain vs. who buys.

**Solution reaction (15 min)** — Show prototype (this repo's `index.html`). "If the AI flagged a clause as high-risk, what would you do next? What would make you ignore it?" Watch for trust thresholds and false-positive tolerance.

**Willingness to pay (5 min)** — "What do you pay today across e-signature, storage, and any CLM tooling? If this replaced two of those, what would a fair price be?"

## Evidence standards

- A pain point counts as validated when ≥60% of a segment describes it unprompted.
- Solution signal requires behavioral evidence (asks for beta access, offers to intro their team), not politeness ("that's cool").
- Every interview is synthesized within 48 hours into an opportunity-solution tree; assumptions log updated weekly.

## Key risky assumptions being tested

1. **Trust:** counsel will act on AI flags rather than re-reviewing everything anyway. (Kills the ROI story if false.)
2. **Integration reality:** operators will connect their e-signature vendor rather than demand we replace it.
3. **Buyer alignment:** the VP Ops budget covers workflow tooling; if this must come from legal's budget, sales motion changes.

## Complementary quantitative signals

- Product usage funnel from beta (intake → AI review viewed → flag acted on → executed).
- Time-in-stage distributions before/after SLA nudges (pre/post comparison per design partner).
- Win/loss notes from sales on deals where transaction management was the trigger.
